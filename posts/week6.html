Week 6 - In-Depth with Backgrounds

While continuing to imagine and implement improvements to the blog, scrolling through extended content may be less distracting if the background were to remain still. Initial attempts to create this effect were fruitless. Instead of giving the body element a background, an img was inserted as the first child, its position was set to absolute, and it was sized to fill the screen. All this did was cause the background not to repeat, the image still scrolled upward with the content.

After some brief research, the solution turned out to be rather underwhelming. CSS already contains attributes that control this aspect of content display, and these two rules can be applied to the body to achieve the desired effect:

  • background-repeat: no-repeat;
  • background-attachment: fixed;

As you can currently see, the background remains motionless as the user scrolls through the content. I believe the appearance to be an improvement. The investigation itself, however, was still lacking. It may perhaps be valuable to explore different tricks and applications of content backgrounds.

Most of these background attributes would likely be most appropriate for what they were used for above, interesting background effects for content on the page, rather than for the page itself. These properties could even be used in clever ways to display content. Notice that it is possible for an element to display multiple images without containing a single child element.
